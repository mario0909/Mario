It is not a rare thing for curiosity to allure the everyday routine. Often, we embark ourselves in inner voyages of indulging curiosity. This voyages may come in the form of a daydream, a dejavu or an “Eureka” moment. Whatever they may be, in most of the times they shade light to new ideas.

 

I’m not sure what my inner voyage was. I am sure though that it was during an amble walking through the sunlit streets of Gold Coast. The other detail I can recall are stars. Now, you may easily assume that this may have been the effects of a heat stress, normal for walking the streets of Gold Coast, but having had one before I can assure you that this was different. It felt more like a dance. As if a tango was being performed inside my Brain. I had abandoned the true way. The irrational thoughts (the poetic side) intertwining with the rational (what would my mother think of this). Anyway, during 3 minute inner voyage, different from Dante Alighieri I managed to bring out one idea and this reflective text (he brought three books). Allow me to walk you through it.

 

 

Universe, has amazed me since I was a child. After food, reading and biography movies, stargazing is the other thing that touches me profoundly. I have read somewhere that It transforms the astronauts life and it does too to the curious one behind the home telescope. You could easily argue that if we all watched the stars we could become more sensitive in the way we behave in planet earth.

 

Here on planet earth we are used to be touched by different ‘scapes’. Whether walking through land-scape, snorkeling in the richness of coral sea-scape or listening to new tunes on soundscape, we humans seem to have found the place to heal or find answers to ourselves.  I have grown to believe that similarly starscape is part of this picturesque. You could easily compare hours of hiking with time spent gazing throughout the universe. All part of the nature in the end. The challenge to us is, can we design for stargazing? Yes, you’ve gusset it. In that three minute voyage I dreamed of an open space observatory. It was built somewhere in the city, and people collectively were watching the depths of the universe. I could see hearts pierced with joy and love for planet earth.

 

While building an open star observatory of may seem an utopia now, experience has suggested two main things;

Some ideas are easier to explain, comment or criticise than they are to realise (Open space observatory). Then, there are other ideas which are extremely easy to realise but very hard to comment. I came to think that introducing ‘Starscape’ as part pf our public realm it is not an impossible task because of lack in technology rather because we haven’t challenged ourselves enough to publicly explore skies. Stars remain without a doubt a quest for humans to understand who we are in the Universe.
